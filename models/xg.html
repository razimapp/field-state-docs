Shot model

xG estimates the quality of a shot.

It answers a simple question: if this shot is taken from here, under these conditions, how often does it become a goal?

Overview Models Data Roadmap

How it works

Train a model on shot location, body part, angle, pressure, and assist context.

Where it helps

Shot quality, chance creation, finishing analysis, and post-shot review.

Scenario fit

Best when the what-if choice leads to a shot or changes shot quality later in the possession.

How to test it

Use calibration curves, Brier score, and time-based splits. Check if higher xG shots score more often.

What it misses

xG does not value the whole possession by itself. It starts late in the sequence.

Investor wording

“xG tells us how dangerous a shot really was, which is useful after the action becomes a shooting chance.”
