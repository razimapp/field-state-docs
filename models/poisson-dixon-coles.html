Score model

Poisson and Dixon-Coles are built for score prediction.

They are the classic tools for match results, scorelines, and tournament-style forecasting.

Overview Models Data Roadmap

How it works

Estimate the goal rate for each team, then turn that into a scoreline distribution.

Where it helps

Match result probabilities, correct score markets, over/under, and tournament simulation.

Scenario fit

Best for the open-football style product and any score-first product.

How to test it

Use log-loss, calibration, and draw accuracy. Dixon-Coles is useful when low scores matter.

What it misses

It is strong for outcomes but weak for action-level detail and tactical branching.

Investor wording

“This is the classic score model, useful when the product focus is match prediction.”
