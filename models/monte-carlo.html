Branch model

Monte Carlo simulates many possible football futures.

It is the best way to show uncertainty. Instead of one answer, it gives a range of likely branches.

Overview Models Data Roadmap

How it works

Run the same scenario many times with probabilities for each next event.

Where it helps

“Most likely next 10 events”, score ranges, comeback chances, and scenario confidence.

Scenario fit

Ideal once the transition model gives you the next-action probabilities.

How to test it

Compare simulated distributions with real match distributions. Check whether the branches look believable.

What it misses

It is only as good as the probabilities underneath it. Bad inputs give noisy branches.

Investor wording

“This lets us show the range of possible football outcomes, not just one forecast.”
