What makes Fieldstate different

Not just match analysis. What-if football intelligence.

Most tools help users understand what happened. Fieldstate is being built to let users adjust a football decision and estimate how the next phase may change.

Staged Ambition

The pass room opens first

MVP should focus on pass decisions. Penalties, corners, free kicks, tracking and neural simulation become later modules with their own data and gates.

Category difference

Traditional Analysis

What happened?

  • Watch, tag, search and clip.
  • Review match events and video.
  • Compare teams, players and patterns.
  • Useful, but mainly descriptive.
Fieldstate

What if the decision changed?

  • Edit the pass or action.
  • Compare actual vs alternative.
  • Estimate completion, value and risk.
  • Show outcome paths with evidence gates.

Traditional tools explain the match played. Fieldstate tests the match that could have been played.

Staged capability path

Stage 1

Pass Decision MVP

Select one pass, edit the endpoint, compare probability, value, risk and continuation.

Investor meaning

Proves the product loop without pretending to simulate all football.

Stage 2

Pass Analysis Product

Rank pass moments, review players and teams, export analyst reports, show evidence gates.

Investor meaning

Becomes pilot-ready for analysts, clubs and coaching conversations.

Stage 3

Wider Decision Intelligence

Add carries, turnovers, momentum, possession value and similar-moment search.

Investor meaning

Expands from pass tool into a decision-intelligence platform.

Stage 4

Set-Piece Modules

Penalties, corners and free kicks get dedicated data, labels and models.

Investor meaning

Discrete premium modules: coachable, repeatable and easy to understand.

Stage 5

Full What-If Engine

Tracking/video-informed movement, neural sequence models and richer scenario simulation.

Investor meaning

The full football decision operating system.
