# Option A - NewCo Founder-Led

This is a discussion draft, not a legal agreement or legal advice. It describes a founder-led company structure where Fieldstate IP ultimately sits inside a clean company vehicle.


Not legal advice. Subject to counsel review.

## Plain-English summary

A new company is formed for Fieldstate. The company owns or receives the Fieldstate IP once founder, investor, role, and protection documents are agreed. Razim remains founding technical/product lead with meaningful founder economics. Investor funds the company and receives agreed equity or instrument rights.

## Commercial purpose

This route is best when the investor wants a long-term investable company rather than a one-off commissioned build. It gives the investor clean company ownership while keeping Razim aligned as the person who built and will continue to deliver the product.

## What investor gets

- Investment into a clean company vehicle.
- Company ownership of Fieldstate IP after agreed assignment/licence mechanics.
- A founder/operator committed to delivery through defined sprint and roadmap milestones.
- Defined governance, reporting, and milestone review.
- Clear route to future data, model, pilot, and platform funding stages.

## What Razim keeps/protects

- Meaningful founder economics, exact percentage to be agreed in the term sheet.
- Founder/operator role and retainer while actively delivering.
- Protection against assigning IP before company documents protect his role and rights.
- Background tooling and generic methods carve-out, subject to counsel drafting.
- Good-leaver protection if removed without cause.

## Indicative cost breakdown

| Item | Indicative range | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Stage 1 feasibility funding | GBP 15k-30k | Funds MVP v2 feasibility sprint and investor/pilot asset. |
| Founder/operator retainer | GBP 6.4k-12k/month | Depends on time commitment, founder terms, exclusivity, and scope. |
| Later pilot/product funding | GBP 30k-120k+ | Separate from Stage 1; tied to delivery and next milestones. |

| Legal/company setup | Separately budgeted | Counsel, tax, accounting, corporate formation, shareholder documents. |

## IP mechanics

- During formation, Razim retains ownership unless and until assignment/licence terms are signed.
- Fieldstate IP is assigned or licensed into NewCo only after company documents are agreed.
- IP should go to NewCo, not an individual person.
- Any assignment should preserve agreed background tooling carve-outs.
- If investment or company formation does not complete, Razim retains the IP unless otherwise agreed in writing.

## Investor protection

- Founder vesting can lock Razim into delivery.
- Milestones can control funding tranches.
- Company receives defined rights to use the product once documents complete.
- Board/observer/reporting arrangements can be agreed.
- Counsel can structure investor rights through equity, SAFE/convertible, or shareholder agreement.

## Razim protection

- No automatic IP assignment before protective company documents exist.
- No broad non-compete beyond narrowly defined Fieldstate-specific obligations.
- No IP sale or exclusive licence without agreed consent/protection.
- Good-leaver acceleration or equivalent protection if removed without cause.
- Retainer continues while Razim is actively delivering.

## Open issues for counsel

- Exact company structure and jurisdiction.
- Founder vesting, leaver provisions, and acceleration.
- IP assignment versus exclusive licence into NewCo.
- Consent rights on IP sale or exclusive licence.
- Investor instrument type and future round compatibility.
- Treatment of open-data attribution and third-party licences.
