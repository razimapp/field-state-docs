ML model

XGBoost helps rank football outcomes from structured features.

It is a good fit when you already know the match state in numbers: rating gap, form, minute, score, home advantage, lineups, and other structured inputs.

Overview Models Data Roadmap

How it works

XGBoost builds many small decision trees. Each tree fixes mistakes from the last one.

The result is strong at learning non-linear patterns without needing perfect manual rules.

Where it helps

Pre-match win/draw/loss, in-play win probability, scoreline classes, and market-style heads.

Good scenario fit

Use it when the question is: given this state, what is the most likely football outcome?

How to test it

Use time-based splits. Train on earlier matches, validate on later matches, and test on unseen periods.

Check log-loss, calibration, and stability across seasons or tournaments.

What it does not do well

It does not naturally understand causal football structure. It predicts from features you give it.

Investor wording

“This is a strong structured-data model for ranking likely football outcomes from a match state.”
