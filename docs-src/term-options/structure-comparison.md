# Fieldstate Structure Options - Comparison

This document compares four commercial structures for funding and owning Fieldstate. It is a discussion draft, not a legal agreement or legal advice. All terms are indicative and subject to counsel review. Not legal advice.

## Executive summary

There are four clean routes:

1. **Option A - NewCo Founder-Led:** best if the investor wants a long-term company asset and Razim remains founder/operator.
2. **Option B - Build + Transfer Option:** best if investor wants first right to buy while Razim keeps IP during the build.
3. **Option C - Full IP Assignment:** best if investor wants asset acquisition or work-for-hire style ownership, priced accordingly.
4. **Option D - Equal Founder Optics + Build Option:** best if Vlad needs visible founder-level protection while investor still gets a staged path to larger ownership.

Retainer pays for delivery time. IP transfer or company ownership is a separate commercial term.

## Investor economics: which route is the strongest deal for the investor?

Judged purely on investor risk and return, **Option B is the strongest of the three**:

- **Lowest cost of being wrong.** The investor's exposure at every point is only the
  rungs already funded — and each rung delivered a real asset (demo, pilot, data,
  models). Options A and C both require committing at the point of maximum
  uncertainty: A prices equity on day one; C pays the full asset price before the
  market has proven anything.
- **Highest value of being right.** Each rung is bought at a **pre-agreed pricing
  formula**, not a renegotiated market price. If Fieldstate performs, the value
  created between rungs accrues to the investor on exercise — the formula locks in
  tomorrow's position at terms agreed today. Option A gives no path beyond the
  initial stake; Option C captures all upside but only by paying peak price for
  unproven work upfront.
- **Control without the acquisition premium.** Option B reaches the same destination
  as Option C — majority control, even full acquisition — but pays for it in stages,
  each priced after evidence, with exclusivity blocking any competing buyer for the
  cost of an option fee.
- **The founder stays locked in and motivated the whole way.** Under Option C the
  builder is paid out and gone; under Option B the founder's equity vests through
  every rung the investor funds — the investor's money and the founder's upside point
  the same direction until the end.

Option C is only the better deal if the investor is already certain today that
Fieldstate succeeds — in which case the ladder's rung-5 acquisition delivers the same
ownership with several evidence checkpoints in between. Option A is the better deal
only for an investor who wants a conventional minority position and no control path.

## What all options exclude unless stated

- Licensed football data fees.
- Advanced neural model training and GPU campaign costs.
- Legal fees, tax advice, accounting, company formation, and due diligence.
- Production infrastructure beyond the defined MVP or sprint scope.
- Commercial guarantees, club adoption guarantees, or model accuracy guarantees.

## Recommended use

Use this comparison to choose the commercial shape before discussing detailed legal drafting. If the investor wants low equity for Razim, the fair alternative is higher cash, narrower rights, or delayed IP transfer.
