Five-stage capability roadmap

From pass decision MVP to the full football what-if engine.

This document shows how product capability expands after each stage earns the right to proceed. It does not define the first sprint contract, budget or commercial route.

Roadmap discipline

Capability follows evidence

Each stage has a user outcome, a data requirement and a gate. Future stages remain future until those requirements are funded and validated.

Stage map

1. Pass Decision MVPOne recorded pass decision, one controlled alternative and evidence-backed explanation.
2. Pilot-Ready Pass AnalysisRepeatable match and player workflows, saved moments, reports and pilot onboarding.
3. Data-Rich Decision IntelligenceBroader decision types, pressure, momentum, possession value and longitudinal profiles.
4. Set-Piece ModulesSpecialist penalty, corner and free-kick review with module-specific evidence.
5. Full Football What-If EngineTracking/video layers and richer spatial or sequence scenario modelling.

How capability grows

Stage 1 · Current foundation

Pass Decision MVP

Review a recorded pass, identify visible options, edit one target and explain the estimated trade-off with clear provenance.

Gate to advance

The workflow is reliable, understandable and evidence-backed; unsupported outputs remain excluded.

Stage 2 · Pilot target

Pilot-Ready Pass Analysis Product

Add ranked moments, saved reviews, player and team reports, repeatable analyst workflows and controlled pilot onboarding.

Gate to advance

Pilot users can repeat the workflow, understand the outputs and identify decisions worth acting on.

Stage 3 · Funded data depth

Data-Rich Decision Intelligence

Expand beyond pass selection into carries, turnovers, pressure, chance creation, momentum, possession value and player trends.

Gate to advance

Approved datasets provide sufficient coverage, and each added model clears its own held-out and calibration checks.

Stage 4 · Specialist modules

Set-Piece Modules

Create distinct workflows for penalties, corners and free kicks, including role preparation, rehearsal and review.

Gate to advance

Each module has appropriate labels, evidence and football validation rather than reusing open-play assumptions.

Stage 5 · Dream product

Full Football What-If Engine

Combine licensed tracking or video context with richer spatial and sequence models for broader scenario exploration.

Gate to advance

Data rights, model validation, security, compute, monitoring and user value justify production investment.

Stage boundaries

Do not pull the future into the MVP

The first stage proves one decision workflow. It does not absorb set pieces, full tracking, every persona workflow or neural simulation.

Do not promote models by novelty

A later model enters the product only when its coverage, reliability and calibration justify the user-facing claim.

Do not scale before repeatability

Infrastructure, team and operational complexity should follow demonstrated workflow value and a clear stage need.

Roadmap rule

Every stage should have a measurable user outcome, an explicit evidence gate and a documented decision to continue, revise or stop.
