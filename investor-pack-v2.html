Football decision intelligence

Turn match moments into better football decisions.

Fieldstate helps analysts, coaches, managers and players review what happened, understand the options available, and test carefully labelled alternatives.

The immediate funding case is deliberately focused: turn the working prototype into a controlled, credible and repeatable product asset before funding broader data and model expansion.

Current position

Working base, disciplined next step

  • Real event-data replay and pass-chain views.
  • Controlled pass and carry scenario edits.
  • Event-only pass-completion evidence across 113,598 passes, 105 matches and 5 competitions.
  • Recorded fact, model estimate and hypothetical output kept visibly separate.

Why Fieldstate

Football organisations have growing volumes of match data, but the useful question is still human: what decision was made, what other option existed, and what should be learned from it?

Review

Make moments explainable

Start from recorded actions and present the relevant sequence, context and available evidence clearly.

Compare

Measure decision trade-offs

Use gated probability and value signals to compare choices without presenting estimates as facts.

Improve

Build a learning loop

Connect match review, analyst evidence, coaching discussion and player reflection over time.

Why now

The prototype proves the workflow

Fieldstate already demonstrates real moment selection, replay, scenario editing and evidence-led model reporting. The next risk is delivery quality, not whether the product idea can be shown.

A focused sprint controls cost

The first funded step can improve reliability, usability, packaging and proof without making licensed tracking data or neural modelling a dependency.

Execution and technical leadership

The build is managed as a staged technical programme with explicit scope, measurable acceptance criteria and evidence gates.

Small, testable releases

Delivery is broken into reviewable outputs with clear definitions of done, rather than one large speculative build.

Source-controlled evidence

Code, model artifacts, decisions and public documentation are versioned so claims can be traced and reviewed.

Risk is made visible

Security, data rights, test coverage, technical debt and unsupported model capability are recorded as delivery constraints, not hidden assumptions.
